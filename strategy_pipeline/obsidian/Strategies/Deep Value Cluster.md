## Overview

Deep value strategies are the philosophical opposite of trend following: buy what is cheap and hated, hold until the market recognizes fair value. Lauer uses a 6-screen fundamental process (EV/EBITDA, P/E, insider buying, cash flow). Okumus buys stocks down 60-90% from highs with improving fundamentals. Greenblatt's Magic Formula (high ROIC + low EV/EBITDA) is the most systematic value approach. Platt trades credit using deep fundamental analysis of individual companies. All are equity/credit-specific, fundamentals-driven strategies requiring deep company analysis.

## Core Rules

1. **Buy cheap assets with improving fundamentals.** All four traders screen for undervaluation by some metric (P/E, EV/EBITDA, distance from highs).
2. **Concentrated positions (5-15 stocks).** Lauer and Okumus are concentrated deep value. Greenblatt is more diversified (20-30).
3. **Time horizon is months to years.** These are not trading strategies — they are investment approaches. Okumus holds for months; Greenblatt holds for 1 year.
4. **Catalyst identification improves timing.** Lauer requires an identifiable catalyst (earnings surprise, management change, sector rotation) before entering.
5. **Risk management varies widely.** Okumus uses 10-15% stops. Greenblatt holds regardless of price movement (no stops). This is the widest risk management divergence of any cluster.
