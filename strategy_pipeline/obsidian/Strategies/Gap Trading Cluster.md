## Overview

Chan documents two opposing gap phenomena: US equity gaps tend to mean-revert (buy the gap-down, short the gap-up — Sharpe 1.5 on SPX components), while European index gaps (FSTX) show momentum continuation (trade with the gap — Sharpe 1.4). The difference is attributed to information content: European gaps at open reflect overnight US news (genuine information), while US equity gaps are often order-flow imbalances. Lien's forex gap strategies are session-specific: Asian-to-London gaps often reverse, while London-to-NY gaps tend to continue.

No strategy files were generated for this cluster as gap trading requires specialized execution infrastructure (pre-market data, opening auction dynamics).

## Core Rules

1. **US equity gaps tend to mean-revert.** Buy gap-downs, short gap-ups. Sharpe 1.5 on SPX components. Best performance is intraday-only (close position by EOD).
2. **European index gaps tend to continue.** FSTX opening gaps reflect real overnight information. Trade with the gap direction. Sharpe 1.4.
3. **The market and instrument determine gap behavior.** You cannot apply a single gap strategy across all markets.
4. **Forex gaps are session transitions, not overnight.** Lien documents that forex "gaps" occur at session boundaries (Asian → London, London → NY) rather than true overnight gaps.
5. **Intraday holding is standard.** Both Chan and Lien close gap trades within the session.
