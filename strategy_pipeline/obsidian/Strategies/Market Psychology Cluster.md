## Core Principles

1. **Douglas's 5 Fundamental Truths:**
   - Anything can happen
   - You don't need to know what happens next to make money
   - There is random distribution between wins and losses
   - An edge = higher probability, not certainty
   - Every moment in the market is unique

2. **Taleb's warnings against overconfidence:**
   - Survivorship bias: we see winners, not the graveyard of losers
   - Narrative fallacy: we construct stories to explain random outcomes
   - Data mining fallacy: enough backtests will always find "edges" that don't exist
   - Confusing noise for signal: short-term results tell you almost nothing

3. **Losses are the cost of doing business.** Faulkner's NLP reframe. Van Tharp agrees. Douglas: "accept risk without emotional discomfort."

4. **Consistency comes from process, not prediction.** All authors agree: focus on executing the process, not on being right about any single trade.

5. **Self-awareness precedes market awareness.** Krausz: understand your biases and emotional patterns before trading. Van Tharp: mental rehearsal of both winning and losing scenarios.

6. **Mechanical discipline beats discretionary impulse.** Eckhardt, Trout, Basso, and Dennis (across Market Wizards books) all independently conclude that removing human override improves performance.
