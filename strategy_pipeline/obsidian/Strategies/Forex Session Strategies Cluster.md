## Overview

Lien's session-based strategies exploit the structural behavior of forex markets across time zones. The Asian session forms tight ranges (consolidation), London open triggers breakouts (often with initial fake-outs as dealers hunt stops), and the US session provides follow-through. The 8AM-12PM ET US/Europe overlap is identified as peak volatility and the optimal trading window. These strategies target different market microstructure phenomena despite being session-based.

## Core Rules Consensus

1. **Asian session = consolidation range.** Low volatility forms the range. The high and low of the Asian session define the breakout levels.
2. **London open = breakout trigger (with fake-outs).** Institutional flow at London open triggers the real move, but dealers often hunt stops first.
3. **US/Europe overlap = peak opportunity.** 8AM-12PM ET provides the highest volatility and follow-through for breakout trades.
4. **16-20 GMT = dead zone.** Low activity, choppy price action, poor trade quality. Lien's data supports skipping this window.
5. **Fixed pip stops (20-30 pips) and 1:2 R:R.** Lien uses consistent risk parameters across session strategies.

## Key Differences

- **London Breakout focuses on the fake-out/real-deal dynamic; Asian Range Breakout is simpler (just trade the first break)**
- **Double Zero Fade is mean-reversion at round numbers — fundamentally different from the other two**
- **All are forex-specific but the session dynamics apply to Gold which trades in the same global sessions**
