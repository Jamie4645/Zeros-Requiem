## Core Rules Consensus

1. **The edge is in the statistics, not the prediction.** All traders emphasize that no single trade is predictable — the edge emerges over hundreds of trades.
2. **Risk management is paramount.** Trout: tiered limits (1.5% per trade, 4% per day, 10% per month). Chan: half-Kelly for safety. Thorp: mathematical position sizing.
3. **High trade count validates the edge.** Both Trout and Blake need large sample sizes. Chan requires statistical significance before committing capital.
4. **Test stationarity before trading.** Chan emphasizes cointegration/ADF testing. Correlation alone is insufficient — the relationship must be stationary.
5. **Simplicity over complexity.** Trout warns against over-complicated models. Blake's system is remarkably simple. Chan advocates parsimony in parameters.
6. **Adapt to changing market conditions.** Trout adjusts models as microstructure evolves. Chan's Kalman filter provides adaptive hedge ratios.
