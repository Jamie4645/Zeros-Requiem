## Overview

O'Neil's CAN SLIM system is the most completely documented growth stock selection methodology ever published. It combines 7 fundamental and technical factors into a single decision framework: buy the best growth stocks breaking out of sound chart bases during confirmed market uptrends, cut all losses at 7-8%, and let winners run. The system was developed from studying every top-performing US stock from 1880-2009.

**Core Edge:** Combine fundamental screening (earnings, sales, institutional sponsorship) with technical chart pattern breakouts (cup-with-handle, double bottom, flat base) and market timing (distribution days, follow-through days). The 7-8% loss cutting rule is the insurance policy. The 3:1 reward/risk ratio means you only need to be right 1 out of 3 times.

### The 7 CAN SLIM Factors

#### C — Current Quarterly Earnings Per Share

```
REQUIRED: Current quarterly EPS up 25-30%+ vs same quarter prior year
PREFERRED: 40-500%+ EPS growth in quarter before breakout
ACCELERATION: Earnings growth rate increasing (15% → 25% → 40%)
REVENUE: Sales also up 25%+ in most recent quarter
WARNING: Two consecutive quarters of major EPS deceleration = bearish
```

#### A — Annual Earnings Increases

```
REQUIRED: Annual EPS growth of 25%+ for each of last 3 years
REQUIRED: Annual ROE of 17%+ minimum
ALTERNATIVE: If ROE < 17%, pre-tax profit margins in top quartile of industry
CONFIRM: Analyst consensus estimates for coming year are UP
```

#### N — New Product, New Management, or New High

```
REQUIRED: Major new product, service, or management driving earnings
REQUIRED: Stock making new 52-week or all-time highs
BUY: As stock emerges from proper base into new high ground
DO NOT: Buy stocks making new lows or in downtrends
DO NOT: Chase more than 5-10% past proper pivot/buy point
```

#### S — Supply and Demand

```
REQUIRED: Volume on breakout day 40-50%+ above 50-day average
BEST: 100-500%+ volume increases on breakout
PATTERN: Volume dries up (decreases) at base lows = selling exhausted
SIGNAL: Heavy volume on up-days + light volume on down-days = accumulation
PLUS: Management ownership, share buybacks
WARNING: Excessive stock splits dilute demand
```

#### L — Leader or Laggard

```
REQUIRED: Relative Price Strength (RS) Rating 80+ minimum
PREFERRED: RS Rating 90+ before major moves
BUY: The #1 or #2 stock in the leading industry group
NEVER: Buy laggard stocks (RS below 70) even if they look "cheap"
AVOID: Sympathy plays (#4 or #5 in a group) rarely work
```

#### I — Institutional Sponsorship

```
REQUIRED: Increasing number of institutional owners over recent quarters
PREFERRED: Quality institutional sponsors (top-performing funds)
BETTER: A few additions by well-known growth funds > hundreds of average funds
AVOID: Stocks with decreasing institutional ownership
```

#### M — Market Direction

```
CRITICAL: 3 out of 4 stocks follow general market direction
RULE: Must determine uptrend vs downtrend BEFORE buying
DETECT TOPS: Count distribution days (see Market Direction Model)
DETECT BOTTOMS: Look for follow-through days (see Follow-Through Day Signal)
ACTION: Go to cash during bear markets — sitting out IS a valid strategy
```

### Entry Checklist (All Must Be Met)

```
1. Market is in confirmed uptrend (follow-through day confirmed)
2. Current quarterly EPS up 25%+
3. Annual EPS up 25%+ for last 3 years
4. Sales growth 25%+ in most recent quarter
5. ROE 17%+ or expanding profit margins
6. RS Rating 80+ (preferably 90+)
7. Stock forming or breaking out of proper base pattern
8. Base is 1st or 2nd stage (avoid 3rd+)
9. Volume on breakout 40-50%+ above 50-day average
10. Stock is #1 or #2 leader in its industry group
11. Industry group in top 40 of 197 groups
12. Increasing institutional sponsorship
13. New product, management, or condition driving growth
14. Buy at pivot point, not more than 5% above it
15. Stock making new 52-week or all-time highs
```

## Strategy 2: Cup-with-Handle Base Pattern

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Chart Pattern Breakout |
| **Timeframe** | Daily / Weekly |
| **Markets** | US Equities |
| **Entry** | Buy at handle high + $0.10 on volume 40-50%+ above 50-day avg |
| **Exit** | 7-8% stop loss; 20-25% profit target |
| **Position Size** | Full at Stage 1-2; reduced at Stage 3+ |
| **Stop Loss** | 7-8% below purchase price |
| **Win Rate** | Most reliable base pattern per O'Neil |
| **Clarity** | 10/10 |

### Pattern Specifications

```
CUP:
    Duration: 7-65 weeks (typical 7-28)
    Depth: 12-33% correction (up to 50% in bear markets)
    Shape: Rounded U-shape (NOT sharp V-bottom)

HANDLE:
    Duration: 1-4 weeks
    Depth: 8-12% correction maximum
    Position: MUST form in UPPER HALF of the cup
    Drift: Should drift DOWN along price lows (wedging up = bad)
    Volume: Should DRY UP (selling pressure exhausted)

BREAKOUT:
    Pivot = Highest price in handle + $0.10
    Volume: 40-50%+ above 50-day average on breakout day
    Chase limit: Do not buy more than 5% above pivot
```

### Volume Signature

```
LEFT SIDE:   Heavy volume on decline
BASE LOWS:   Volume decreases / dries up
RIGHT SIDE:  Volume increases on recovery
HANDLE:      Volume dries up again
BREAKOUT:    Volume 40-50%+ above 50-day average
```

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Market Timing Entry |
| **Timeframe** | Daily |
| **Markets** | US Indices |
| **Entry** | Major index up 1%+ on higher volume than prior day, occurring day 4-7 of rally attempt |

| **Position Size** | Start at 25% invested; scale up as breakouts work |
| **Stop Loss** | Failure of follow-through = back to cash |
| **Win Rate** | ~70-75% of follow-through days lead to sustained rallies |
| **Clarity** | 10/10 |

### Follow-Through Day Logic (Pseudocode)

```
STEP 1: Market makes significant low (index closes at new low)
STEP 2: Day 1 = first day index closes HIGHER (rally attempt begins)
STEP 3: Days 2-3 = index must NOT make new lows (if it does, RESET count)
STEP 4: Day 4-7 = one major index gains 1.0%+ on volume HIGHER than prior day
STEP 5: THIS is the follow-through day → begin buying leading stocks

NOTES:
    Follow-through on Day 4-5 is more powerful than Day 6-7
    NASDAQ follow-through often more significant than S&P
    Multiple indices confirming simultaneously strengthens signal
    EVERY bull market started with a follow-through day
    But ~25-30% of follow-through days fail
```

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Type** | Market Timing Exit |
| **Timeframe** | Daily |
| **Markets** | US Indices |
| **Entry** | N/A — exit signal |
| **Exit** | Sell on accumulated distribution day count |
| **Position Size** | Reduce as distribution accumulates |
| **Stop Loss** | 5-6+ distribution days = go to cash |
| **Win Rate** | Catches most major market tops |
| **Clarity** | 9/10 |

### Distribution Day Rules (Pseudocode)

```
DEFINITION: Index closes DOWN 0.2%+ on volume HIGHER than prior day
STALLING: Index up slightly but heavy volume, gives back gains = counts as distribution

EXPIRATION:
    Distribution days expire after 25 trading days
    OR if index rallies 5%+ from that day's close

ACTION LEVELS:
    3-4 distribution days in 3-4 weeks: Tighten stops, reduce new buying
    5 distribution days in 4-5 weeks: Sell weakest positions, go to 50%+ cash
    6+ distribution days clustered: Sell everything, go to cash

KEY INSIGHT: CLUSTERING matters more than count
    5 days in 2 weeks = WORSE than 5 days spread over 5 weeks
```

### Normal Profit Taking

```
TAKE MOST PROFITS at 20-25% gain from buy point
R/R MATH: 25% gains and 8% losses = need only 1 win in 3 trades to break even
```

### 8-Week Hold Rule (Critical for Mega-Winners)

```
IF stock gains 20%+ in LESS than 3 weeks from breakout:
    HOLD for minimum 8 weeks from breakout date
    DO NOT sell during this period
    AFTER 8 weeks: evaluate whether to continue holding

WHY: Stocks gaining 20% in <3 weeks have high probability of becoming 100-200%+ winners
```

### Climax Top Sell Signals (Exit on ANY)

```
1. LARGEST daily price run since breakout (exhaustion buying)
2. HEAVIEST daily volume since breakout but price stalls (distribution)
3. Exhaustion gap up on huge volume after extended advance (final move)
4. Surge of 25-50% in 2-3 weeks after already being extended
5. Railroad tracks: 2+ heavy-volume days with no price progress (churning)
6. Break above upper channel line (overextension)
7. New price high but RS line fails to make new high (bearish divergence)
8. Weekly volume increase with little price progress (heavy distribution)
9. Stock 70-100% above 200-day MA (extremely overextended)
10. 3rd or 4th stage base breakout fails (late-stage failure)
```

### Additional Sell Signals

- New highs on dramatically lower volume (no demand)
- Stock drops below 50-day MA on heavy volume, fails to recover in 1-2 days
- Below 10-week MA for 8+ consecutive weeks
- Key trendline broken on heavy volume
- Biggest weekly decline since beginning of advance
